Understanding the Struggle

The "Why" Behind Reading Challenges

Reading is a complex skill, and many factors can contribute to a child's difficulties. It's often not a single issue but a combination of things. Common reasons include gaps in foundational skills like phonemic awareness, phonics, or fluency. It's important to remember that a struggle with reading doesn't reflect a lack of intelligence or effort.

What to Look For: A Checklist

  • Struggles with sounding out new words.
  • Slow, choppy reading that lacks rhythm or expression.
  • Avoids reading aloud or gets frustrated easily.
  • Has trouble recalling what was just read.
  • Confuses similar-looking letters and words (e.g., "b" and "d", "was" and "saw").
